Problem · Action · Result
Problem
Women with frequent weight changes had to buy new swimwear every season.

Conventional swimwear is built around S/M/L, so pregnant and plus-size women were forced into repeat purchases.
I saw an empty market for a one-size fit spanning 55–99 kg.

Action
I designed the product, content and operations myself, then improved with data.
① Product PlanningVisited the Chinese factory in person to select crinkle fabric, and refined the strap, padding and patterns through repeated fit tests across 10+ body types.
② Marketing PlanningSet the USP “swimwear you wear without size worries,” personally managed the Wadiz Q&A tab, and planned & produced 100% of the brand’s short-form content.
③ Data-Driven ImprovementAfter the first analysis, cut the color options from 7 to 4 to reduce decision fatigue, and added cover-up styling content to improve purchase conversion.
④ Pop-up & SNS OpsPlanned and ran the Sinchon pop-up store, and verified awareness and demand through SNS comment events.
Result
A repeatable, data-driven improvement cycle.

Arc’teryx Trim Order & Cost Optimization
Arc’teryx trim purchasing & overseas supplier management

Owned Arc’teryx trim purchase orders, managing supply schedules and logistics costs with overseas suppliers.

71% logistics cost reduction
A full breakdown of air-freight costs showed that about 40% came not from production delays, but from an administrative bottleneck — waiting on advance-payment approvals.
I built a Payment Tracking Chart to track every payment from approval to remittance and flag at-risk orders early, cutting air-freight cost from $3,500 (SS26) to $1,000 (FW26).
Automated production scheduling
I built an automated Excel tracker that reflects weekly production-schedule changes and country-by-country lead times. By auto-calculating each supplier’s arrival dates, it eliminated 2–3 hours of manual work and responds faster to material requests and schedule changes.
