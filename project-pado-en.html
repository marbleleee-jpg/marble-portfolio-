Problem · Action · Result
Problem
Women with frequent weight changes had to buy new swimwear every season.

Conventional swimwear is built around S/M/L, so pregnant and plus-size women were forced into repeat purchases.
I saw an empty market for a one-size fit spanning 55–99 kg.

Action
I designed the product, content and operations myself, then improved with data.
① Product PlanningVisited the Chinese factory in person to select crinkle fabric, and refined straps, padding and patterns through repeated fit tests across 10+ body types.
② Marketing PlanningSet the USP “swimwear you wear without size worries,” managed the Wadiz Q&A tab, and planned & produced 100% of the brand’s short-form content.
③ Data-Driven ImprovementAfter the first analysis, cut colors from 7 to 4 to reduce decision fatigue, and added cover-up styling content to improve conversion.
④ Pop-up & SNS OpsPlanned and ran the Sinchon pop-up store, and verified awareness and demand through SNS comment events.
Result
A repeatable, data-driven improvement cycle — proven again at re-funding.

What I Learned
01
Data drives the next decision

First-round data justified cutting colors and adding styling content; the second round clearing 400% again confirmed the direction was right.

02
Consistency builds a brand

Keeping one line — “without size worries” — across product patterns, landing page, content and the pop-up experience is what built consumer trust.

03
Leading means clarifying roles

On a 4-person cross-national team, clearly sharing roles, schedule and standards is what determined the quality and speed of the output.
